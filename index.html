Hello! I'm Cornelius Wolff, a PhD researcher at the TRL Lab at the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica (CWI) and the University of Amsterdam, under the supervision of Madelon Hulsebos and Maarten de Rijke. My work centers on Insight Retrieval from Structured Data, where I investigate AI models and pipelines that can deal with all kinds of structured data like databases and CSV files. I'm furthermore deeply interested in AI in education, In-Context Learning, and efficient machine learning.

Publications.

SQALE: A Large Text-to-SQL Corpus Grounded in Real Schemas

AI for Tabular Data Workshop @ NeurIPS 2025 — Wolff, Gomm & Hulsebos

A large-scale semi-synthetic text-to-SQL corpus with 517,676 validated triples grounded in 135,875 real-world database schemas, enabling more realistic training and evaluation of text-to-SQL models.

READ THE PAPER ↗

Are We Asking the Right Questions? On Ambiguity in Natural Language Queries for Tabular Data Analysis

AI for Tabular Data Workshop @ NeurIPS 2025 — Gomm, Wolff & Hulsebos

A conceptual framework for characterising ambiguity in natural-language queries over tabular data, arguing for cooperative query specification and analysing 15 common tabular-data benchmarks.

READ THE PAPER ↗

MinervAI: Using Generative AI to Assist, Not Replace Humans in Peer Review

AI in Science (AIS) 2025, Copenhagen — Hüsing, Petersen, Weiher, Wolff & Musslick

A position paper arguing for constrained, verifiable uses of LLMs in peer review, introducing MinervAI — an open-source tool supporting citation verification and argumentation mapping while preserving human judgment.

READ THE PAPER ↗

How well do LLMs reason over tabular data, really?

4th Table Representation Workshop – ACL 2025 — Wolff & Hulsebos

A critical examination of LLM reasoning capabilities on tabular data. Identifies flaws in current evaluation methods and proposes an LLM-as-a-judge approach that reveals significant performance deficits under realistic conditions such as missing values and duplicate entities.

READ THE PAPER ↗

PictSure: Pretraining Embeddings Matters for In-Context Learning Image Classifiers

arXiv preprint, 2025 — Schiesser, Wolff, Haas & Pukrop

Demonstrates the critical importance of pretraining embeddings for in-context learning image classifiers, showing how proper embedding pretraining significantly improves performance and generalization capabilities.

READ THE PAPER ↗

GRASP: A Novel Benchmark for Evaluating Language Grounding and Situated Physics Understanding in Multimodal Language Models

IJCAI 2024 — Jassim, Holubar, Richter, Wolff, Ohmer & Bruni

Introduces GRASP, a benchmark designed to evaluate language grounding and situated physics understanding in multimodal language models, assessing how well models can understand and reason about physical interactions in visual contexts.

Bidirectional Emergent Language in Situated Environments

arXiv preprint, 2024 — Wolff, Mayer, Bruni & Ohmer

Investigates bidirectional emergent language in situated environments, exploring how agents develop communication systems through interaction to facilitate coordination in complex, multi-agent scenarios.

A Review of Nine Physics Engines for Reinforcement Learning Research

arXiv preprint, 2024 — Kaup, Wolff, Hwang, Mayer & Bruni

A comprehensive review of nine physics engines commonly used in reinforcement learning research, analyzing their capabilities, performance characteristics, and suitability for various RL applications.

A New Approach on Estimating Germany's Mobile Broadband Coverage based on Crowdsourced Data

27th ITG-Symposium on Mobile Communication, 2023 — Wolff, Tessmer & Aschenbruck

A novel approach for estimating mobile broadband coverage in Germany using crowdsourced data, providing more accurate and up-to-date coverage information compared to traditional measurement approaches.

Master's Study Project — Osnabrück University · July 2022 – September 2024

Study Project: LLMs for Science

Master's Study Project — Osnabrück University · 2024

Managed and organized a study project on "Supporting the Review Process with AI." The AI-driven system automates source validation by verifying citation accuracy against original sources, facilitates literature discovery, and transforms papers into knowledge graphs for structural analysis. Collaborated with IMWI (Prof. Dr. Oliver Thomas) and the Autonomous Empirical Research Group (Sebastian Musslick).

How well do LLMs reason over tabular data?

Online Talk — AlphaXiv · Amsterdam, Netherlands · September 3, 2025

Presented findings on whether general-purpose LLMs can effectively reason over tabular data, identifying flaws in current evaluation methods and proposing an LLM-as-a-judge approach. Demonstrated how real-world characteristics like missing values and duplicate entities substantially impair LLM tabular reasoning.
